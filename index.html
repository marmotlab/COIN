Simulations

1. Comprehensive experiments

During formation switching, the WMR formations encounter obstacles and is able to avoid them while switching to the new formation. Our method demonstrates strong adaptability and flexibility to real-world environments. When the need for formation switching is identified, we leverage LLMs to understand the environment and task requirements, and then reason to generate a new desired formation.

2. Formation navigation with dense obstacles

Formation navigating through dense obstacles. Our spatio-temporal joint optimization trajectory planning method demonstrates strong coordination and multi-objective optimization capabilities, making it well-suited for such challenging environments.

Real-world experiments

1. Formation navigation

Seven robots arranged in three columns move in a hexagonal formation.

2. Autonomous obstacle avoidance during formation navigation

The two outermost robots balance formation maintenance and obstacle avoidance simultaneously. This capability enhances the adaptability and flexibility of the WMR formations in real-world environments, as the surroundings are not always open and free of obstacles.

3. Formation switching

The formation of seven WMRs switches from three columns to two. Such switching enhances the WMR formations' adaptability to real-world environments. For example, sudden changes in navigable areas represent one type of environments where the formation must adjust accordingly.

Abstract

Formation control of wheeled mobile robots (WMRs) has been extensively studied for its applications in many fields such as logistics transportation and resource exploration. However, previous work has mainly focused on simple scenarios with fixed formation configurations, which significantly limits their flexibility and adaptability in real-world environments with obstacles. To address this, we propose a semi-distributed formation control approach for WMRs that combines centralized formation generation with distributed trajectory planning. We leverage the pre-trained generalized understanding and reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) to comprehend complex real-world environments and dynamic tasks, adaptively generating new desired formations. We also present a distributed formation trajectory planning method that effectively coordinates each WMR’s spatio-temporal relationships with others during formation navigation, and efficiently balances conflicting objectives, such as formation maintenance and obstacle avoidance, via integrated optimization. Simulations and real-world experiments confirm the effectiveness and superiority of our approach over advanced baselines. Experiment videos are available at https://dongjh20.github.io/WMRs-Formation-with-LLM.
